[size=150][b]ForgePlayer (beta) — one player, every screen and your e-stim, all in sync[/b][/size]

[img]https://forgeplayer.app/forgeplayer_hero.png[/img]

Ever tried to watch a scene across two screens while an e-stim track plays — and spent the whole time fighting windows that drift out of sync? [b]ForgePlayer[/b] is the fix. It's a free desktop player that drives [b]your monitors and your e-stim / haptic devices from one timeline[/b]. One seek bar. Everything stays locked together, frame for frame.

You point it at a scene, hit play, and:

[list]
[*][b]Video plays across up to three monitors[/b] at once, all in sync — main screen, a companion angle, a second wall, however you've set it up. Each screen can letterbox or crop-to-fill (handy for ultrawides) and go fullscreen on its own monitor.
[*][b]E-stim / haptics play right alongside the video[/b], driven either from a [b]funscript[/b] (synthesised live) or a [b]pre-rendered audio file[/b]. It supports [b]three-phase stereostim[/b] output for modern e-stim hardware, plus a classic continuous waveform for 312 / 2B-style boxes.
[*][b]Each stim output needs its own USB audio dongle[/b] — separate from your speakers. One drives your main stim; an optional second drives a second device (e.g. a prostate channel). Tested with a [url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/B08LGPKFN5]VENTION USB sound card[/url] and others — any standard USB audio output works.
[*]It opens [b].forge scene bundles[/b] with a double-click — the packs exported by [url=https://funscriptforge.com]FunscriptForge[/url], our companion authoring app — and also reads loose funscript / audio folders.
[/list]

It's built for the cockpit-style way people actually watch: big screen in front of you, controls on a laptop or a little touchscreen off to the side, hands free.

[size=125][b]What you need (e-stim hardware)[/b][/size]

You'll want [b]one or two USB audio dongles + one or two stim power boxes[/b]. Haptic 1 carries the three-phase / stereostim signal; Haptic 2 an optional prostate signal.

[b]Three-phase / stereostim boxes[/b] (use Pulse-based mode): [url=https://www.stimkits.com/]The Tingler StimKit I[/url] · [url=https://shop.impudicus.net/products/estim-hero-stereo-basic]EstimHero[/url] · [url=https://darkmatter69.com/collections/estim]ZC95 MKII[/url]

[b]Classic boxes[/b] (use Continuous mode): [url=https://erostek.com/products/mk-312bt-power-unit]MK-312BT[/url] · [url=https://estim.store/collections/2b]2B[/url]

[b]Not yet supported:[/b] Coyote (DG-Lab) — it's Bluetooth, which isn't tested yet.

[size=125][b]This is a beta[/b][/size]

ForgePlayer is [b]pre-1.0 software, actively developed[/b]. It works and it's fun, but expect rough edges — please report anything weird. A few honest caveats:

[list]
[*][b]Windows is the tested platform.[/b] macOS and Linux builds exist but are [b]not yet tested[/b].
[*][b]Bluetooth devices haven't been tested yet.[/b] Today's path is audio-based e-stim (a stim box fed from an audio output).
[*]e-stim safety: [b]keep electrode placement below the waist, start low, and ramp up.[/b] Use the in-app Calibrate button to set a comfortable level before you press play.
[/list]
